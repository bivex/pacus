# Organizational Roles, Responsibilities & Authorities — pacus

**Version**: 1.0  
**Effective Date**: 2026-05-01  

## Overview

This document defines the roles, responsibilities, and authorities for persons doing work under the control of the pacus Quality Management System (QMS).

## Roles & Responsibilities

### 1. Top Management (Project Owner)

**Responsibilities**:
- Demonstrate leadership and commitment to the QMS
- Establish, communicate, and maintain the Quality Policy
- Ensure quality objectives are established at relevant levels
- Integrate QMS requirements into business processes
- Ensure resources needed for QMS are available
- Promote process approach and risk-based thinking
- Communicate importance of effective QMS and conformity
- Ensure QMS achieves intended results
- Engage, direct, and support persons doing work
- Promote improvement and support other management roles

**Authorities**:
- Approve changes to QMS scope and structure
- Allocate resources for quality activities
- Review QMS performance at planned intervals
- Approve quality policy and objectives

---

### 2. Development Team (Software Engineers)

**Responsibilities**:
- Implement and maintain code that conforms to requirements
- Follow coding standards and best practices
- Write and maintain tests (unit, integration, mutation)
- Ensure database schema constraints are properly implemented
- Document code and maintain documented information
- Report nonconformities and participate in corrective actions
- Use risk-based thinking when planning changes

**Authorities**:
- Propose changes to code and schema
- Select appropriate implementation approaches
- Flag quality concerns and nonconformities
- Review and approve merge requests (peer review)

---

### 3. Quality Assurance (QA / Code Review)

**Responsibilities**:
- Plan and conduct internal audits (including mutation testing)
- Evaluate QMS performance and effectiveness
- Verify conformity of products and services
- Monitor customer satisfaction through feedback
- Analyze nonconformities and verify corrective actions
- Maintain audit records and documented information
- Report QMS performance to top management

**Authorities**:
- Access all QMS documented information
- Require evidence of conformity
- Recommend corrective actions
- Block releases that don't meet quality standards

---

### 4. Database Administrator (DBA)

**Responsibilities**:
- Design, implement, and maintain database schemas
- Ensure referential integrity (foreign keys, triggers)
- Implement append-only audit trails and status history
- Control database access and protect data integrity
- Monitor database performance and availability
- Execute backup and recovery procedures
- Maintain documented information on schema changes

**Authorities**:
- Approve schema changes and migrations
- Control database user access
- Define and enforce data retention policies
- Validate backup and recovery procedures

---

### 5. Release Manager

**Responsibilities**:
- Control release of products and services
- Verify planned arrangements are satisfactorily completed
- Ensure release criteria are met before deployment
- Maintain records of releases and approvals
- Coordinate post-delivery activities
- Communicate release status to stakeholders

**Authorities**:
- Approve or block releases
- Require evidence of conformity before release
- Authorize emergency fixes with appropriate controls

---

## Competence Requirements

All roles require:
- Appropriate education, training, and/or experience
- Awareness of quality policy and relevant objectives
- Understanding of their contribution to QMS effectiveness
- Knowledge of implications of nonconformity

Competence is evaluated regularly, and actions are taken to acquire or improve competence as needed. Evidence of competence is retained as documented information.

---

## Communication of Roles

- This document is available in the project repository
- Roles and responsibilities are communicated during onboarding
- Updates are communicated to all affected persons
- Responsibilities are assigned explicitly in project management tools
